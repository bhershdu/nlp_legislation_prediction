METHODOLOGY:

This bill, effective January 1, 2023, amends RSA 265:102, IV, by adding a fine of a minimum of  $300 for a first offense, a fine of $500 for a second offense, and third or subsequent offense shall receive a suspension of a person’s driver license for a period of 90 days. The assumption is the suspension will be applied upon conviction, meaning that once a person indicates a plea of guilty, the Division of Motor Vehicles will provide a notice via first class mail to the driver notifying the driver their privilege to drive will be suspended in 30 days from the date of the notice for a period of 90 days for a third offense or subsequent offense. For each third or subsequent violation under this bill, a driver would be subject to the suspension of operating privileges and for each restoration there is an associated $100 fee.

 

While this bill would increase state highway fund fine revenue, the amount is indeterminable as there is no mechanism in place to calculate how many tickets would be issued in the future for littering, nor is there a way to calculate how many third or subsequent tickets would be issued resulting in restoration fees.
