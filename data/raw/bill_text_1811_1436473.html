AN ACT\trelative to bingo dates.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1  Bingo Licenses; Game Dates.  Amend RSA 287-E:6, II(a)-(b) to read as follows:

\t\t\t(a) No more than one license shall be issued to a charitable organization per calendar month, and the license shall permit no more than [10] 16 game dates of bingo in one calendar month, and in no more than one town or city which has authorized bingo games under RSA 287-E:27. The license shall expire on the last game date authorized under said license.

\t\t\t(b) The provisions of subparagraph II(a) notwithstanding, a license may be issued to a charitable organization per fiscal year, and the license shall permit no more than [10] 16 game dates of bingo in one calendar month and no more than [120] 192 game dates of bingo in one calendar year. The license shall expire on the last game date authorized under the license. Charitable organizations electing to receive an annual license under this paragraph shall be ineligible to receive concurrently a monthly license under subparagraph II(a), and charitable organizations licensed under subparagraph II(a) shall be ineligible to receive concurrently an annual license under this subparagraph.

\t2  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect July 1, 2021.

METHODOLOGY:

This bill would increase the allowable number of bingo game dates offered by charitable gaming organizations.  The Lottery Commission states the license fee for a bingo event is $25 per game date and there are currently 200 charities offering bingo.  While unable to predict how many additional bingo game dates may be added as a result of this bill, the Commission states the increase in state revenue per year would range from $0 (no new game dates added) to $360,000 (all 200 charities offer bingo up to the bill’s maximum).  Lottery revenue is credited to the lottery fund, with net revenues after expenditures being credited to the state education trust fund.  This bill is not expected to increase state expenditures.
