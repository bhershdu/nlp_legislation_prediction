ANALYSIS

 

\tThis bill provides a mechanism for the nonprobate transfer of land, done by permitting owners of interests in real property to execute and record a transfer on death (TOD) deed.

STATE OF NEW HAMPSHIRE

 

In the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand Twenty Two

 

AN ACT\tpermitting transfer on death deeds for real property.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1  New Chapter; Uniform Real Property Transfer on Death Act.  Amend RSA by inserting after chapter 563-C the following new chapter:

CHAPTER 563-D

UNIFORM REAL PROPERTY TRANSFER ON DEATH ACT

\t563-D:1 Short Title. This chapter may be cited as the Uniform Real Property Transfer on Death Act.

\t563-D:2 Definitions. In this chapter:

\t\tI. “Beneficiary” means a person that receives property under a transfer on death deed.

\t\tII.  “Designated beneficiary” means a person designated to receive property in a transfer on death deed.

\t\tIII.  “Joint owner” means an individual who owns property concurrently with one or more other individuals with a right of survivorship. The term includes a joint tenant, owner of community property with a right of survivorship, and tenant by the entirety. The term does not include a tenant in common or owner of community property without a right of survivorship.

\t\tIV.  “Person” means an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership, limited liability company, association, joint venture, public corporation, government or governmental subdivision, agency, or instrumentality, or any other legal or commercial entity.

\t\tV. “Property” means an interest in real property located in this state which is transferable on the death of the owner.

\t\tVI.  “Transfer on death deed” means a deed authorized under this chapter.

\t\tVII. “Transferor” means an individual who makes a transfer on death deed.

\t563-D:3 Applicability. This chapter applies to a transfer on death deed made before, on, or after the effective date of this chapter by a transferor dying on or after the effective date of this chapter.

\t563-D:4 Nonexclusivity. This chapter does not affect any method of transferring property otherwise permitted under the law of this state.

\t563-D:5 Transfer on Death Deed Authorized. An individual may transfer property to one or more beneficiaries effective at the transferor’s death by a transfer on death deed.

\t563-D:6 Transfer on Death Deed Revocable. A transfer on death deed is revocable even if the deed or another instrument contains a contrary provision.

\t563-D:7 Transfer on Death Deed Nontestamentary. A transfer on death deed is nontestamentary.

\t563-D:8 Capacity of Transferor. The capacity required to make or revoke a transfer on death deed is the same as the capacity required to make a will.

\t563-D:9 Requirements. A transfer on death deed:

\t\tI.  Except as otherwise provided in paragraph II, shall contain the essential elements and formalities of a properly recordable inter vivos deed;

\t\tII.  Shall state that the transfer to the designated beneficiary is to occur at the transferor’s death; and

\t\tIII.  Shall be recorded before the transferor’s death in the public records in the office of the county register of deeds of the county where the property is located.

\t563-D:10 Notice, Delivery, Acceptance, Consideration Not Required. A transfer on death deed is effective without:

\t\tI.  Notice or delivery to or acceptance by the designated beneficiary during the transferor’s life; or

\t\tII.  Consideration.

\t563-D:11  Revocation by Instrument Authorized; Revocation by Act not Permitted.

\t\tI.(a) Subject to subparagraph (b), an instrument is effective to revoke a recorded transfer on death deed, or any part of it, only if the instrument:

\t\t\t\t(1) Is one of the following:

\t\t\t\t\t(A) A transfer on death deed that revokes the deed or part of the deed

expressly or by inconsistency;

\t\t\t\t\t(B) An instrument of revocation that expressly revokes the deed or part of the deed; or

\t\t\t\t\t(C) An inter vivos deed that expressly revokes the transfer on death deed or part of the deed; and

\t\t\t\t(2) Is acknowledged by the transferor after the acknowledgment of the deed being revoked and recorded before the transferor’s death in the public records in the office of the county register of deeds of the county where the deed is recorded.

\t\t\t(b) If a transfer on death deed is made by more than one transferor:

\t\t\t\t(1) Revocation by a transferor does not affect the deed as to the interest of another transferor; and

\t\t\t\t(2) A deed of joint owners is revoked only if it is revoked by all of the living joint owners.

\t\tII.  After a transfer on death deed is recorded, it may not be revoked by a revocatory act on the deed.

\t\tIII. This section does not limit the effect of an inter vivos transfer of the property.

\t563-D:12  Effect of Transfer on Death Deed During Transferor’s Life.  During a transferor’s life, a transfer on death deed does not:

\t\tI.  Affect an interest or right of the transferor or any other owner, including the right to transfer or encumber the property;

\t\tII.  Affect an interest or right of a transferee, even if the transferee has actual or constructive notice of the deed;

\t\tIII.  Affect an interest or right of a secured or unsecured creditor or future creditor of the transferor, even if the creditor has actual or constructive notice of the deed;

\t\tIV.  Affect the transferor’s or designated beneficiary’s eligibility for any form of public assistance;

\t\tV.  Create a legal or equitable interest in favor of the designated beneficiary; or

\t\tVI.  Subject the property to claims or process of a creditor of the designated beneficiary.

\t563-D:13  Effect of Transfer on Death Deed at Transferor’s Death.

\t\tI.  Except as otherwise provided in the transfer on death deed, in this section, or in statutes governing antilapse, revocation by divorce or homicide, survival and simultaneous death, and elective share, if applicable to nonprobate transfers, on the death of the transferor, the following rules apply to property that is the subject of a transfer on death deed and owned by the transferor at death:

\t\t\t(a)  Subject to subparagraph (b), the interest in the property is transferred to the designated beneficiary in accordance with the deed.

\t\t\t(b)  The interest of a designated beneficiary is contingent on the designated beneficiary surviving the transferor.  The interest of a designated beneficiary that fails to survive the transferor lapses.

\t\t\t(c)  Subject to subparagraph (d), concurrent interests are transferred to the beneficiaries in equal and undivided shares with no right of survivorship.

\t\t\t(d)  If the transferor has identified 2 or more designated beneficiaries to receive concurrent interests in the property, the share of one which lapses or fails for any reason is transferred to the other, or to the others in proportion to the interest of each in the remaining part of the property held concurrently.

\t\tII.  Subject to RSA 477, a beneficiary takes the property subject to all conveyances, encumbrances, assignments, contracts, mortgages, liens, and other interests to which the property is subject at the transferor’s death.  For purposes of this section and RSA 477, the recording of the transfer on death deed is deemed to have occurred at the transferor’s death.

\t\tIII.  If a transferor is a joint owner and is:

\t\t\t(a)  Survived by one or more other joint owners, the property that is the subject of a transfer on death deed belongs to the surviving joint owner or owners with right of survivorship; or

\t\t\t(b)  The last surviving joint owner, the transfer on death deed is effective.

\t\tIV.  A transfer on death deed transfers property without covenant or warranty of title even if the deed contains a contrary provision.

\t563-D:14  Disclaimer.  A beneficiary may disclaim all or part of the beneficiary’s interest as provided by RSA 563-B, but shall do so within 60 days of the transferor's death.  The beneficiary shall record a copy of the disclaimer in the registry of deeds of the county in which the property or interest disclaimed is located.

\t563-D:15  Liability For Creditor Claims And Statutory Allowances.

\t\tI.  To the extent the transferor’s probate estate is insufficient to satisfy an allowed claim against the estate or a statutory allowance to a surviving spouse or child, the estate may enforce the liability against property transferred at the transferor’s death by a transfer on death deed.

\t\tII.  If more than one property is transferred by one or more transfer on death deeds, the liability under paragraph I is apportioned among the properties in proportion to their net values at the transferor’s death.

\t\tIII.  A proceeding to enforce the liability under this section shall be commenced not later than 18 months after the transferor’s death.

\t2  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect January 1, 2023.
