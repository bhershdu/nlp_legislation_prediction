AN ACT\testablishing an education freedom account program administrator in the department of education and making an appropriation therefor.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1 New Section; Department of Education; New Position; Education Freedom Account Administrator. Amend RSA 21-N by inserting after section 6-a, the following new section:

\t21-N:6-b Education Freedom Account Administrator. There is established within the division of learner support the position of education freedom account administrator who shall be a classified employee. The education freedom account administrator shall be qualified to hold such position by reason of education and experience. The position shall be subject to any other employment requirements as determined by the department.  The education freedom account administrator shall coordinate and provide technical assistance to guide students, parents, and the scholarship organizations responsible for dispensing the education freedom accounts (EFAs) under RSA 194-F.  The education freedom account administrator shall also serve as a resource for administrators, educators, families, scholarship organizations, and policymakers across the state.

\t2  Appropriation.  The sum of $115,000 for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2023 is hereby appropriated to the department of education, division of learner support, for the position of education freedom account administrator as established in RSA 21-N:6-b.  The governor is authorized to draw a warrant for said sums out of any money in the treasury not otherwise appropriated.

\t 3  Authority and Responsibilities of the Scholarship Organization; Education Freedom Accounts.  Amend RSA 194-F:4, V to read as follows:

\t\tV.(a)  The scholarship organization may withhold from deposits or deduct from EFAs an amount to cover the costs of administering the EFA program, up to a maximum of 10 percent annually.

\t\t\t(b)  For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2025 and the fiscal year ending June 30, 2026, the department of education may withhold up to 50 percent of the prior year costs associated with the education freedom account administrator established in RSA 21-N:7-a.  The department shall calculate the total costs under this section and provide this amount to be withheld to the scholarship organization no later than August 15 of each year.  The scholarship organization shall include the amount withheld under subparagraph (a), in the calculation of costs of administering the EFA program, which shall not exceed 10 percent annually of the EFAs approved.

\t\t\t(c)  For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year thereafter, the department of education may withhold 100 percent of the prior year costs associated with the education freedom account administrator established in RSA 21-N:7-a.  The department shall calculate the total costs under this section and provide this amount to be withheld to the scholarship organization no later than August 15 of each year.  The scholarship organization shall include the amount withheld under subparagraph (a) in the calculation of costs of administering the EFA program, which shall not exceed 10 percent annually of the EFAs approved.

\t4  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect July 1, 2022.

METHODOLOGY:

This bill creates an education freedom account administrator position within the Department of Education and provides an appropriation therefor.  The Department anticipates this position would be labor grade 29, with an estimated total cost (salary, benefits, other) of $115,000 in FY 2023, $113,000 in FY 2024, and $118,000 in FY 2025, assuming a July 1, 2022, start date.
