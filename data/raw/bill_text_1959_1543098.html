AN ACT\trelative to days of operation of state liquor stores.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1  Sunday and Holiday Openings.  Amend RSA 177:5 to read as follows:

\t177:5 Sunday and Holiday Openings. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the commission may, in its discretion, open for business on any Sunday or legal holiday any state liquor store located on a main route where traffic is heavy[; provided, however, that no state liquor store may be opened for business at any time on Easter or Thanksgiving day, whenever appointed, and Christmas day]. Although full-time employees shall be given the option of working, no such employee shall be required to work on such days. The commission may employ part-time employees, subject to the rules of the director of personnel, to staff stores open on Sundays or holidays. Any full-time employee who works on such days shall be paid 1-1/2 times the employee's regular rate of pay for the actual number of hours worked.

\t2  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect 60 days after its passage.

METHODOLOGY:

This bill changes the regulation on places of operation of state liquor stores and allows state liquor stores to be open on Easter.

 

The NH Liquor Commission provided the following information concerning the fiscal impact of this bill:

    \r\n\t\t\t
  • The Commission does not operate any stores located within 200 feet of a church, chapel, or parish house.  The proposed change in sections 1 and 2 of the bill would have no fiscal impact.
  • Regarding the change which would allow liquor stores to operate on Easter Sunday, the Commission provided the following estimates:
      \r\n\t\t\t\t
    • The total gross revenue generated on a Sunday in March or April is approximately $1 million.
    • In March and April of 2021, the average personnel cost for stores open on Sunday was approximately $48,000.
    \r\n\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t

 

Any balance remaining in the Liquor Fund after paying all expenses of the commission is deposited in the general fund.

 

It is assumed that any fiscal impact would occur after FY 2022.
