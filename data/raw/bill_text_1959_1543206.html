STATE OF NEW HAMPSHIRE

 

In the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand Twenty Two

 

AN ACT\trelative to a likeness of Wentworth Cheswill at the state house.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1  Declaration of Purpose.  Mr. Wentworth Cheswill was born on April 11, 1746 in Newmarket, New Hampshire.  Mr. Cheswill, a man of color, has been said to have been the first African-American elected official in the United States.  He was a Revolutionary War veteran and served under John Langdon in Langdon's Company of Light Horse Volunteers.  He was an elected Newmarket town official and served the town in various elected town offices from 1768 until his death in 1817.  In 1801, Mr. Cheswill was among the founders of the first free library in the town and provided in his will for public access to his personal library.

\t2  Commemorative Historical Plaque for Wentworth Cheswill.  The joint legislative historical committee is authorized to accept a gift of a commemorative historical plaque for Wentworth Cheswill and oversee the placement of the plaque in the state house.

\t3  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect July 1, 2022.
