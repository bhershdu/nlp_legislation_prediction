STATE OF NEW HAMPSHIRE

 

In the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand Twenty Two

 

AN ACT\trelative to unemployment benefits for employees terminated for refusing to comply with a vaccine mandate.

 

Be it Enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court convened:

 

\t1  New Subdivision; Vaccination Mandates.  Amend RSA 275 by inserting after section 77 the following new subdivision:

Vaccination Mandates

\t275:78  Vaccination Mandates.  An employee who is terminated, placed on leave, or given zero hours of billable time, for noncompliance with an employer-mandated vaccination requirement shall have the right to:

\t\tI.  An additional 6 months of standard unemployment benefits under RSA 282-A:25 beyond the maximum duration or benefit amount, payable by the employer or the department of unemployment security.

\t\tII.  An expedited court hearing which shall occur not more than 14 days after the filing of a claim under this subdivision.  

\t\tIII.  Recover court costs and legal fees incurred in enforcing this subdivision.

\t2  Effective Date.  This act shall take effect 60 days after its passage.
