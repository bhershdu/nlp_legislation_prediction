HOUSE BILL\t566

 

AN ACT\trelative to the discussion of disclosure of the minutes from a nonpublic session under the right-to-know law.

 

SPONSORS:\tRep. Yokela, Rock. 33; Rep. Yakubovich, Merr. 24

 

COMMITTEE:\tJudiciary

 

-----------------------------------------------------------------

 

AMENDED ANALYSIS

 

\tThis bill:

 

\tI.  Requires that sealed minutes in nonpublic sessions under the right-to-know law may be unsealed if in the opinion of a majority of members voting in nonpublic session determine that circumstances have changed.  The minutes sealed pursuant to RSA 91-A:3, II(d) would become public after one year.

 

\tII.  Provides for the appointment of counsel for a minor in a juvenile delinquency proceeding.

 

\tIII.  Provides for recovery of unauthorized payments by the state, as requested by the department of health and human services.

 

\tIV.  Provides for alternative dispute resolution.

 

\tV.  Permits judicial referees to issue orders in non-contested probate matters.

 

\tVI.  Permits a supreme court justice to sit as a circuit court judge.

 

\tVII.  Provides for payment of costs for services other than counsel for indigent parties.

 

\tVIII.  Requires a penalty assessment on violations.

 

\tIX.  Provides for cybersecurity incident reporting and recommended cybersecurity standards for political subdivisions.

 

\tX.  Provides for emergency medical and trauma services data.

 

\tXI.  Provides for the appeal of a claim denied by the victims' assistance commission.

 

\tXII.  Provides for insurance company licenses.

 

\tXIII.  Establishes the New Hampshire National Guard enlistment incentive program.

\t34  Reference to National Guard Scholarship Fund Removed.  Amend RSA 110-B:55, I to read as follows:

\t\tI.  Fines may be paid to a military court or to an officer executing its process.  The amount of any fine imposed may be noted upon any state roll or account for pay of the delinquent and deducted from any pay or allowance due or thereafter to become due them, until said fine is liquidated; or the same may be collected with lawful costs of collection, as in the case of executions issued in action founded upon torts.  [Fines shall be paid over to the state treasurer and credited to the New Hampshire national guard recruitment and retention scholarship fund under RSA 110-B:60.]

\t35  Reference to National Guard Scholarship Fund Removed.  Amend RSA 110-B:29 to read as follows:

\t110-B:29  Use of Armories or Other National Guard Facilities.  

\t\t[I.]  All New Hampshire national guard facilities shall be primarily for the military duty, instruction, and training of the national and state guard and for the storage and maintenance of military property.  Other use of national guard facilities may be authorized by the adjutant general and shall be governed by rules and regulations promulgated under this section.

\t\t[II.  Rental fees for the use of national guard facilities shall be fixed by the adjutant general and shall be declared as revenue and paid to the adjutant general subject to the provisions of RSA 110-B:61.]
