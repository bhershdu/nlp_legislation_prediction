STATE OF NEW HAMPSHIRE

 

In the Year of Our Lord Two Thousand Twenty Two

 

A RESOLUTION\topposing all federal and state efforts to establish a carbon tax on fuels for electricity and transportation.

 

\tWHEREAS, affordable and abundant electricity is a strategic resource that is essential to modern life and America’s economic competitiveness; and

\tWHEREAS, the application of a carbon tax to gasoline and other transportation fuels will have a dramatic, immediate impact on transportation costs; and

\tWHEREAS, a carbon tax, by design, will result in substantial, immediate increases in the price of electricity, making electricity less affordable for millions of Americans; and

\tWHEREAS, a carbon tax in the United States will have no impact on China, India, and other major sources of carbon emissions throughout the world, except to increase their competitiveness relative to the United States; and

\tWHEREAS, a carbon tax will put United States exporters and manufacturers at a competitive disadvantage by increasing domestic manufacturing production costs; and

\tWHEREAS, the sole purpose of the tax code should be to fund necessary government functions rather than to manipulate consumer decisions; now, therefore, be it

\tResolved by the House of Representatives:

\tThat the state of New Hampshire opposes all federal and state efforts to establish a carbon tax on fuels for electricity and transportation; and

\tThat copies of this resolution, signed by the speaker of the house of representatives be forwarded to each member of the New Hampshire congressional delegation.
